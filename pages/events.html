Women's History Month is celebrated every March - but why is it important? What does it mean?
We celebrate Women's History Month to remind ourselves of the accomplishments of women throughout the years to our culture and society . From science to politics, it's is a chance to reflect on the trailblazing women who lead the way for change. Today, the SPEAK TO LEAD CLUB with the support of English Language teachers Guțu Ludmila and Elena Magu has explored the vital role of women for human civilization throughout modern history, including the impact of World War I on women's rights and the granting of voting rights to women. We also highlighted the inappropriate stereotypes for both girls and boys, the presence of gender inequality that exists today. Or, the way we celebrate women in our own lives must always be unique. Women, of course, are also a part of history for the other 11 months of the year. And I would add that women were influencing the course of history long before 1980, or 1911. Nevertheless, I am glad that there is, each year, a time when we pause to think about the role of women because history is more than simply what happened, and more than what women and men did to make it happen. History is what we remember about those deeds. It is the lessons we have taken from the past and the knowledge we draw on to live today and to shape the future.
